New to Government of Canada travel

If you’re a first-time traveller for the Government of Canada (GC) or need a refresher, this section will inform you about the GC travel system and available travel resources.

Sign up for an Amex Global Business Travel account

American Express Global Business Travel (AmexGBT) is the official supplier of the Government of Canada’s travel management solution. To access the system, create an AmexGBT account to plan your trip, book tickets and create an expense claim.

Getting started with Government of Canada business travel

Create an Amex Global Business Travel account

If you’re travelling or booking a trip for someone else on behalf of the Government of Canada, you need to create an Amex Global Business Travel (AmexGBT) account and complete a traveller profile.

For instructions on how to create your AmexGBT account and complete your traveller profile, refer to Registering for the Shared Travel Services portal (PDF, 2.0 MB).

Apply for a travel card

The Individual Designated Travel Card (IDTC) is a credit card employees can use to pay for travel-related expenses. These expenses include accommodations, car rentals, meals and other incidental expenses. To avoid using your personal credit card when travelling on behalf of the Government of Canada, apply for an IDTC.

IDTC cardholders must follow the Individual Designated Travel Card terms and conditions (PDF, 170 KB) and benefit from worry-free insurance coverage under Rental Vehicles Insurance.

Apply for a new IDTC by Applying for an Individual Designated Travel Card. Then follow the link to the Bank of Montreal (BMO) online eapplication to begin the process through BMO’s portal. You will receive an email once your Departmental Travel Card Coordinator (DTCC) approves your application. Paper application forms are to be used only in exceptional circumstances or by employees in international locations.

Your card may take up to 6 weeks to arrive.

Helpful resources about Government of Canada business travel

Travelling for business on behalf of the Government of Canada requires you to use the AmexGBT travel management solution. AmexGBT used to be named after the Hogg Robinson Group company (or HRG) so you may here some colleagues refer to the travel management system as HRG. Rest assured HRG and AmexGBT are the same system, just with a new name. Once you’ve created an AmexGBT account, reference the following training material to get started.

  • Introduction to Government of Canada Business Travel: a brief overview of travelling on behalf of the GC, booking travel for someone else or approving travel requests and expense reports
  • Completing a traveller profile: a walk-through of the best practices for completing a travel profile which will be used in the Online Booking Tool
  • Creating a travel itinerary: an online course to help GC employees use the AmexGBT travel management solution to research, book travel and create a travel request
  • Managing travel expenses: an online course to help GC employees use the AmexGBT travel management solution to create an expense report for travel related expenses
  • Approving travel requests and expense reports: an online course to help GC employees use the AmexGBT travel management solution to complete the approval process for travel requests and expense reports.
