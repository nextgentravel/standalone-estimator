Frequently asked questions

Below you will find some frequently asked questions about:

  • Booking a trip
  • Booking a hotel
  • Expense report
  • Travel policies and directives

Booking a trip

Please consult this information on how to book a trip.

Naming a trip

Name your trip something unique and distinguishable to avoid confusion. We recommend you include the date and destination in the trip name. This can differentiate it from similar trips.

Checking the status of a travel request

If your request has been approved, you will receive a confirmation email. In the Amex Global Business Travel (AmexGBT) travel management solution, you will notice a green checkmark beside your travel request. If you have also requested a travel advance, you will receive an email about funds being transferred to your account as indicated by your travel request within a couple of days.

If your request is rejected, you will receive an email with a link to modify your travel request. If you do not receive an email, review your travel request in the AmexGBT travel management solution . Please check with your approver if you are unsure of what to modify to get your travel request approved.

Cancelling or modifying a travel request

As a traveller or a travel arranger, you can change or cancel your travel request by logging into the AmexGBT travel management solution. Please note that you can cancel your travel request only if it is not yet approved.

Choosing transportation options

The Government of Canada requires that you take the most economical means of travel. Depending on your destination and distance , you may choose between air, rail or car for your trip.

Using your own vehicle for business travel

The Government of Canada Travel Calculator can estimate the amount you can expense based on the estimated kilometers you will travel.

Booking transportation or accommodation outside of the Amex Global Business Travel travel management solution

You must book transportation and accommodation within the AmexGBT travel management solution or by calling the AmexGBT call center directly.

Submitting your travel request in the Amex Global Business Travel management solution when not booking services through the travel system

When using your personal vehicle or staying in private accommodations, you must still submit a travel request.

This applies only to departments who use the AmexGBT Online Booking Tool and Expense Management Tool (EMT). If your department uses an independent expensing tool, contact your Senior Full-Time Travel Officer (SFTO) in your department’s finance team.

Booking a hotel

Please consult the following information when booking a hotel.

Booking a hotel which falls outside of policy

Yes. You can book a hotel above the city rate limit, since external factors can contribute to a higher cost. In these cases, a written explanation for exceeding the city rate limit must be provided to your approver during the trip pre-approval process.

Expense report

Please consult the following information for help with expense reports.

Modifying the approver

You should always verify the approver before submitting your travel request. You can modify your approver by locating your trip in the EMT. Within the Approval tab, you can select a new approver based on the list within the system. If the name of your desired approver does not appear from the dropdown, please contact your Travel Helpdesk or Travel System Administrator.

Modifying a recommender

You should also always verify the recommender before submitting your travel request. You can modify your recommender by locating your trip in the EMT. Within the Approval tab, you can set a different recommender from the list in the system. Once you set a different recommender, please remove any recommender you do not wish to include. When more than 1 recommender is required, add the first recommender on the list. It is the responsibility of the recommender to add any additional recommenders to the approval process if it is required.

Trip costs that exceed previously approved estimates

Depending on your department’s set allowances, their tolerance range, and your justifications, your trip may be approved and booked as is. Otherwise, you may have to restart the booking process to meet the allowed cost.

Reimbursements for private accommodations

If you book private accommodations, include it in the EMT as an out-of-pocket expense. Private accommodations are still subject to National Joint Council’s policy under Appendix D.

Claimable out-of-pocket expenses

For more information related to specific out-of-pocket expenses, please refer to Part 3 - Travel Modules of the National Joint Council Travel Directive.

Who to contact for help with expensing

Please contact your departmental finance team for questions and issues related to reimbursements, setting up deposits or financial coding.

Please contact your Travel Helpdesks or SFTO for questions and issues related to the EMT within the AmexGBT travel management solution.

Modifying the Financial Coding Block

By default, all the Financial Code Blocks (FCBs) are assigned based on the codes in your traveller profile. However, it you might have to modify an FCB when an expense must be assigned to a different financial code than the default.

To assist you, follow section 7. Modify a Financial Coding Block within the Creating an Expense Report guidebook.

Printing an expense report from the Expense Management Tool?

For assistance, you can consult section 13. Print, Export or Delete an Expense Report within the Creating an Expense Report guidebook.

Submitting expenses in the Expense Management Tool without booking through the AmexGBT travel management solution

You must still submit through the EMT if you did not book through the AmexGBT solution. For example, if you used your personal vehicle, you must still submit the kilometric expense within the EMT to be reimbursed.

Travel policies and directives

Please consult the following information for help with travel policies and directives.

Blanket Travel Authorities

A blanket travel authority (BTA) is a continuous or repetitive travel authorization. BTAs have no variation in the specific terms and conditions of trips where it is not practical to get approval from the employer for each individual trip. Any exceptions to the BTA parameters require that the individual trip be specifically pre-approved where possible. BTAs do not apply to groups of employees. Employees may have more than 1 BTA simultaneously.

The BTA is valid for 1 fiscal year and should be renewed every year.

When booking travel in the AmexGBT travel management solution, you can select the blanket travel authority option from a drop-down menu in the Accounting/Payment details page for travellers who have a BTA. The travel request will be approved automatically.

Please note there are no BTAs for travelling as a non-public servant.

BTA approval depends on the departmental delegation of authority. Please contact the designated departmental travel coordinator or your departmental financial policy group for more information.

Note: Departments without the EMT in the AmexGBT travel management solution can only create travel requests with an offline TAN. Therefore, all approval is offline. There is no need for a BTA in the AmexGBT travel management solution as it is out of scope for those departments. Travellers from those departments should contact their SFTO for the specific departmental procedures for BTAs.

Accommodation and Car Rental Directory rates for international travel

Accommodation and Car Rental Directory car rental rates are not approved for international travel. They apply only to domestic and transborder travel.
