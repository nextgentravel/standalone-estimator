Returning from your trip

This section has information on submitting an expense report once your travel is completed.

Note: Not all departments use the integrated expense management tool within the Amex Global Business Travel (AmexGBT) travel management solution. If your department uses an independent expensing tool, please contact your department’s finance team to understand their expensing tool. The information below supports those departments that use the integrated expense management tool within the AmexGBT travel management solution.

Create an expense report

Create an expense report to request reimbursement for expenses incurred during your travel.

Step 1: Prepare receipts

Before creating your expense report:

  1. ensure you have receipts of all expenses (except for meals, which are a flat sum under your per diem)
  2. separate your receipts by expense type
  3. verify your reimbursement payment methods are set up correctly in your profile

Tip: File your expenses as soon as possible, with as much detail as needed. This way the approver and financial support can approve your expense report easily and you can be reimbursed quickly.

Step 2: Create expense report

Add transactions

Credit charges incurred on the Departmental Travel Expense Account (DTEA) and Individual Designated Travel Card (IDTC) cards will automatically populate the Expense Management Tool (EMT). To locate DTEA and IDTC credit card expenses incurred during you travel in the EMT:

  1. 1. select the appropriate credit card account
  2. 2. select the appropriate card statement period
  3. 3. select the appropriate credit card transactions

For further assistance, consult section 4. Add Credit Card Transactions within the Creating an Expense Report guidebook.

Tip: The DTEA card is your department’s travel authorization card that automatically prepays expenses such as air, rail and rental car bookings through the AmexGBT travel management solution. The IDTC is your authorized personal travel credit card to use when on business travel.

Assign expense categories

All credit card transactions that can be expensed need an expense category assigned to them before you can submit them as part of the expense report. For each transaction:

  1. 1. elect an expense type
  2. 2. Enter the amount to be claimed
  3. 3. Enter comments if required

Tip: The selectable expense categories are the ones that you selected during the booking process.

For further assistance, consult section 5. Assign Expense Categories to Credit Card Transactions within the Creating an Expense Report guidebook.

Add and link receipts

Upload your receipts to the EMT, linking each receipt to their respective expense item that you created for your expense report.

For further assistance, consult section 9. Add Receipt Images and section 10. Linking Receipt Images within the Creating an Expense Report guidebook.

Add out-of-pocket expenses

To add out-of-pocket expenses, fill out the relevant expense details:

  1. 1. select the relevant expense type
  2. enter the amount to be claimed
  3. enter the date it was incurred
  4. add comments if required

For further assistance, consult section 6: Add out of pocket expenses within the Creating an Expense Report guidebook.

Tip: Enter the financial codes for each expense, if you have not already done so in your profile.

Step 3: Submit expense report

To submit your expense report:

  1. verify the approver
  2. submit the expense report for approval

For further assistance, you can consult section 12. Verify Approver and Submit Expense Report for Approval within the Creating an Expense Report guidebook.

Tip: If this is your first time travelling, contact the finance team in your department for help with the reimbursement process. This will likely involve a form to provide your banking information. Submit the form as soon as possible to avoid delays in your reimbursement.

Once you’ve submitted your expense report, this final step will link the expenses into 1 report.

  1. 1. Click “Expense Report: Create New Item”
  2. 2. Add any comments for your approver
  3. 3. Save

To assist you, follow section 8 Finalize Expense Report within the Creating an Expense Report guidebook.
