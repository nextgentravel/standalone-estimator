Government travel cards

Below you will find useful documents about IDTCs and DTECs.

Individual Designated Travel Cards

The IDTC is a credit card issued to Government of Canada (GC) business travellers. Travellers should use the IDTC for all authorized travel expenses, including accommodations, vehicle rentals, taxis, meals and other incidental expenses.

Departmental Travel Expense Account

The Departmental Travel Expense Account (DTEA) is a "virtual" credit card used for air, rail, group travel services and related service charges. Each DTEA is linked to a specific Travel Authorization Number (TAN) code. Costs are charged to the DTEA based on the TAN used to make the traveller's booking.

Note: There is no physical card associated with the DTEA.

Departmental Travel Expense Cards

The Departmental Travel Expense Card (DTEC) is a credit card used for larger departmental travel expenses and in special travel circumstances when an IDTC cannot be used. A DTEC must be in the name of an authorized individual within a responsibility center.

Important: Cash withdrawals are not permitted.
